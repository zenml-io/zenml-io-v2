## WandB Pricing Plans Overview

WandB uses a tiered pricing structure that scales with team size and usage requirements. The platform distinguishes between cloud-hosted managed services and self-hosted deployments, with different pricing models for each approach.

WandB uses tracked hours as a primary billing metric for cloud services, where one tracked hour equals one hour of wall-clock time during model training. This means if your model takes 8 hours to train, you consume 8 tracked hours regardless of hardware specifications.

The pricing model avoids per-run fees, giving you predictable costs for experiment management, but usage-based billing can become expensive for teams running extensive parallel training jobs or long-running experiments.

## WandB Pricing Factors to Consider

When budgeting for WandB, keep the following factors in mind:

### 1. Team Size and Seats

WandB‚Äôs paid plans charge per user (seat). The Personal plan allows only 1 user, and each Pro user pays a monthly fee (starting at $50 per month, which is per seat). If your team is large or growing, those per-user fees can add up quickly. On the flip side, WandB does not charge extra for guests.

### 2. Feature Needs vs. Toolchain

WandB excels at experiment tracking and visualization, but it doesn‚Äôt natively include things like pipeline orchestration, hyperparameter sweeps (beyond basic WandB ‚ÄòSweeps‚Äô), or advanced dataset versioning.

If your workflow demands those features, you‚Äôll need additional tools. That can increase total cost and complexity. For example, [using Prefect or Kubeflow alongside WandB](https://www.zenml.io/blog/orchestration-showdown-dagster-vs-prefect-vs-airflow) means paying for multiple platforms.

### 3. Data Storage and Usage Limits

Consider how much data and compute you will log. WandB‚Äôs Free cloud tier has limits; the free plan has 5 GB of storage, after which extra storage costs ~$0.03 per GB per month.

Even paid tiers can incur overage charges for extremely large runs or storage (especially if using the AWS, GCP, or Azure cloud services). If you log massive datasets or long training runs, these fees will significantly increase your usage charges.

### 4. Security and Compliance

Advanced security features for WandB come only in the Enterprise plan. The free and Pro plans lack SSO, audit logs, and some compliance certifications. If you work in regulated industries like healthcare or finance, check that WandB Enterprise supports your requirements (the Enterprise plan offers HIPAA compliance, SOC2, and SAML SSO).

## What's New in WandB for 2025

The WandB platform has evolved significantly in 2025 to address the growing demands of both traditional MLOps and emerging LLMOps workflows. Understanding these new capabilities is essential when evaluating the platform's value proposition.

### W&B Inference: Hosted LLM Access

Launched in 2025, W&B Inference powered by CoreWeave represents a major expansion beyond WandB's traditional experiment tracking focus. This new product provides API and playground access to leading open-source LLMs including OpenAI GPT OSS, Qwen3, Llama 4, DeepSeek, and Phi models‚Äîall without requiring separate hosting providers or API keys.

The key advantage is unified observability: W&B Inference runs directly on CoreWeave infrastructure with monitoring built into W&B Weave, eliminating the fragmented workflows that plague teams juggling multiple LLM providers. Free users receive inference credits for a limited time, after which they must activate pay-as-you-go subscriptions or upgrade to paid plans.

This positions WandB to compete more directly with comprehensive AI development platforms rather than serving solely as an experiment tracker.

### Registry General Availability

W&B Registry reached general availability in 2025, replacing the legacy Model Registry. The new Registry offers enhanced governance capabilities including a Restricted Viewer role, programmatic access control via SCIM, and the ability to star registries for quick access. Organizations are being migrated automatically in stages across server releases.

### Deprecated Features and Migration Requirements

Personal entities (personal accounts) were deprecated in May 2024. Users must now create Teams to access W&B Inference and other modern features. This change affects pricing structure, as the minimum viable deployment now requires team-based accounts rather than individual personal plans.

### Market Context: LLMOps Convergence

The broader MLOps market reached $1.7 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow to $129 billion by 2034 at a 43% CAGR. A key driver is the convergence of traditional MLOps with LLMOps‚Äîspecialized practices for deploying and managing large language models. WandB's expansion into inference hosting and prompt engineering workflows positions it for this trend, but also means organizations need to evaluate whether they want an all-in-one platform or prefer best-of-breed integrations.

## Is WandB Expensive?

Whether WandB is expensive depends on your perspective and needs. The free plan costs nothing, so individuals benefit heavily. The Pro plan at $50 per user per month is a significant investment for larger teams ‚Äì a 10-person team pays around $6,000 per year.

For a small startup, that might seem steep when compared to free (open-source) tools like [MLflow](https://www.zenml.io/blog/metaflow-vs-mlflow) or ZenML.

However, WandB‚Äôs hosted solution eliminates the engineering effort of building your own tracking system. Many enterprises justify the cost as WandB‚Äôs polished UI and reliability can speed up development and collaboration.

By comparison, the Advanced Enterprise (on-prem) plan can be very expensive (high five to six figures annually) since it covers all enterprise requirements. However, for large organizations, this cost is offset by the ROI of having 50+ data scientists easily share experiments and meet compliance.

In short, WandB‚Äôs per-user fees are on the higher side, but may be reasonable for teams that need its convenience and features. If you‚Äôre a smaller team with limited funds, you might find WandB‚Äôs paid plans hard to justify.

Let‚Äôs look at how ZenML can replace or augment key aspects of WandB‚Äôs functionality, often at a more accessible price point.
